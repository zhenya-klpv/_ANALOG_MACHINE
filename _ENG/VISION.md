Not an appliance, not a "tuning shop." Feel, presence, motion — not feeds.

## Why Analog Machine

The world is moving toward artificial intelligence, digital systems, and automation at extraordinary speed. We carry gadgets everywhere — laptops, smartphones, voice assistants. Smart homes, smart rice cookers that talk while they cook. Electric cars are becoming the norm. Even Ferrari built an EV.

All of it is convenient, fast, and impressive. But at some point it starts to weigh on you. You want to turn off notifications, start a real engine, and drive into the canyon — not for speed, but to feel that you're in control of the moment again.

**Analog Machine exists for that.**

We believe in things you feel with your hands. Things with mechanics, weight, sound, smell, resistance, and character. Objects that don't just perform a function — they give quiet pleasure from the act of using them.

Omega Speedmaster. Cartier Santos. Montblanc 146. Pelikan M800. Technics SL-1200. McIntosh amplifier. Olympia Cremina. La Pavoni lever espresso. Hasselblad 500CM. Leica M6. Curta mechanical calculator. S.T. Dupont Ligne 2 lighter. Nagra recorder. Campagnolo and Chris King bicycle builds. Porsche 911. BMW E36 and E39. Audi S6 Avant. Saab Viggen.

What unites them isn't age or price. It's **taste**. They're analog, tactile, honest. Pleasure from touch, sound, movement, small rituals — childlike wonder when an object doesn't just work, but lives with you.

**Analog Machine is about those sensations.**

We build cars not as transport, but as mechanical objects of desire. Cars you want to look at, touch, start, listen to, and drive. Where every detail matters: form, material, fit, sound, steering effort, pedal travel, carbon texture, metal, leather, light.

In a world turning digital, disposable, and contactless — we choose the other direction.

> **Handbuilt in California.**  
> **Made for people who still want to feel something.**

## Visual language

- **Phoebe Philo / Old Céline temperature** — quiet luxury, material-led, architectural; insider taste, not logo flex
- **OEM+ Motorsport minimalism** — like a factory prototype they never released
- Painted carbon, not exposed weave (except controlled accent details)
- Period-correct proportions, no stance, no gratuitous widebody
- Interior: Alcantara, leather, clean lines, hidden modern tech
- Palette: black, stone, cream, subtle green — restrained, not candy or chrome
- Photography: architectural, quiet luxury — not garage chaos

## Anti-patterns

- Another "custom E36" in the Instagram feed
- Project without standards — every build from scratch
- Promises to clients before demo car is ready
- Complexity for complexity's sake (roof carbon first, 600 hp, LS swap)

## Success looks like

Someone sees your E36 and says:

> **"I didn't think an E36 could look and feel this expensive."**

Not the most powerful. Not the most expensive. **The most coherent.**

## Product discipline

Standardize, don't customize:

- 3 build levels
- 12 colors
- 3 interior options
- 2 engines (California-legal)
- 1 platform (E36) until concept is proven

## Limited edition (later)

**12 numbered builds** · E36 · California-built · carbon composite · after hero car + 3–5 clients.
